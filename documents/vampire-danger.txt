In vampire folklore, several things are considered dangerous or lethal to these creatures. These include sunlight, fire, a stake through the heart, decapitation, and certain religious symbols like crucifixes and holy water. Additionally, garlic and running water are often depicted as repelling vampires. 
Here's a more detailed breakdown:

    Sunlight:
    Vampires are often portrayed as being vulnerable to or even destroyed by direct sunlight. 

Fire:
Fire can be used to destroy vampires, often requiring the body to be dismembered and burned before it can regenerate. 
Stake to the Heart:
A wooden stake driven through the heart is a common method of killing a vampire. 
Decapitation:
Beheading a vampire is another way to eliminate them, according to the Twilight Saga Wiki. 
Religious Symbols:
Vampires are often repelled by religious items like crucifixes, holy water, and other symbols of Christianity. 
Garlic:
Garlic is a common deterrent for vampires, often used to ward them off. 
Running Water:
Some folklore suggests that vampires are unable to cross running water. 
Mirrors:
Many folklore traditions suggest that vampires cannot see their reflection in a mirror.
